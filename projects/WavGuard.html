WavGuard — Smart Wakeboard

WavGuard was developed to address one of the most challenging aspects of learning to wakeboard: understanding balance on an unstable, moving surface. For beginners, progress is largely driven by trial and error—while coaches can offer guidance, riders must ultimately learn how their feet distribute pressure across the board in real time. Our team set out to reduce this learning curve by providing immediate, intuitive feedback that helps riders recognize and correct their balance as they ride.

Designed by a team of five engineers, WavGuard is a smart wakeboard system that delivers live visual feedback through integrated LED indicators. The board uses a combination of force-sensitive resistors (FSRs) to measure pressure distribution and an inertial measurement unit (IMU) to track orientation and motion, allowing the system to verify and contextualize rider input. All electronic components are fully padded and embedded for comfort and safety, creating a responsive training tool that supports beginners as they build confidence, balance, and technique while riding on the water.

Features

Wire routing
Low-profile cable channels that prevent snagging and reduce wear.

We routed sensor and LED cabling through molded channels beneath the board's top skin. Channels are rounded to avoid sharp bends and use adhesive-backed foam clamps to prevent chafing during flex.

  • Protected inside foam core, away from rider contact
  • Strain-relief at each connector
  • Service access via removable access plate
LED routing
High-visibility LED strips integrated along the edge and toe/heel zones.

LEDs are segmented per zone and driven by the microcontroller for immediate feedback. Diffusion strip and low-profile bezel make the lights visible but not abrasive.

  • Zone-based color mapping
  • Waterproof silicone encapsulation
  • Low-power PWM dimming for battery longevity
Code logic
Sensor fusion and rule-based feedback for robust in-water response.

The firmware fuses FSR and IMU data, applies filtering and outlier rejection, then maps balance metrics to LED states. The logic prioritizes stability and avoids false positives from wakes.

  • Complementary filter for orientation
  • Debounced pressure thresholds to avoid chatter
  • Configurable sensitivity via serial / Bluetooth
Waterproofing
IP67-rated enclosures and sealed cable glands at all penetrations.

Every electronic area is isolated inside a gasketed enclosure. Potting is used selectively for fragile connectors; pressure equalization membranes prevent condensation.

  • Gasketed electronics box
  • Marine-grade marine connectors on service ports
  • Drain channels to avoid water pooling under the skin
Electronics box
Modular, serviceable enclosure with quick-access fastener and internal mounting plane.

The electronics box houses the MCU, battery, and power management. It's mounted on a floating plate to decouple board flex and uses captive fasteners for field service.

  • Hot-swappable battery bay
  • Captive screws and keyed connectors
  • Integrated status LEDs and debug header
Foot sensors (FSR)
Embedded FSR array provides zonal pressure distribution under the binding.

The FSR layout detects heel vs. toe weighting and lateral shifts. Each sensor is calibrated and the firmware compensates for temperature drift.

  • Zonal sensing with 8 discrete pads
  • Analogue front-end with op-amp buffering
  • Calibration routine accessible via debug interface
