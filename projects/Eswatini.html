Nyakuza Foot Bridge — Eswatini, Africa
Role: Quality Control Manager • Location: Emanyonyaneni & Nsizatje • Year: 2024

I spent eight weeks in Eswatini working alongside a team of Duke classmates, Engineers In Action staff, local masons, and community members to design and construct the Nyakuza Suspended Footbridge—the longest pedestrian bridge in the country at 128 meters. The bridge spans the Ingwavuma River, which becomes impassable during the rainy season, cutting off thousands of residents from schools, markets, and healthcare.

Prior to traveling in-country, I assisted with the bridge’s design during the academic year, participating in engineering review calls and collaborating on structural decisions. While on-site, I served as Quality Control Manager, helping ensure construction accuracy and safety while working directly with community members throughout the build process. Living in Emanyonyaneni and Nsizatje, I was immersed in the local culture and gained a deeper understanding of how engineering solutions can meaningfully improve daily life.
